‚Ä¢ By the early 1980s, Dennis had turned an initial stake of less than

The Experiment

‚Ä¢ Dennis would find a group of people to teach his rules to, and then

have them trade his ideas with his money.

‚Ä¢ The training would last for two weeks and could be repeated over

farms he had visited in Singapore and deciding that he could grow
traders as quickly and efficiently as farm-grown turtles.

‚Ä¢ Dennis placed an ad in The Wall Street Journal and thousands

‚Ä¢ No one knows the exact criteria Dennis used, but the process

included a series of true-or-false questions.

P&F charts

‚Ä¢ Xs signify up moves; Os signify down moves.
‚Ä¢ Construction based on box size and reversal size.
‚Ä¢ Box size is the number of points per X or O.

‚Ä¢ Often set to $1.
‚Ä¢ Higher values are less sensitive to trend reversals.
‚Ä¢ Lower values are more prone to reverse.

‚Ä¢ Reversal size is usually 3.

‚Ä¢ When price reverses by 3, start a new column.
‚Ä¢ If up trend, and price moves down by $3, switch from a

column of Xs to Os.

‚Ä¢ If down trend and price rises $3, switch from Os to Xs.

‚Ä¢ Under EMH, gaps show areas where news is announced and it

‚Ä¢ For an up gap, the opening price will be above the previous day‚Äôs

‚Ä¢ For a down gap, the open will be below the previous day‚Äôs low and

Gaps signal important market action

‚Ä¢ If news explains the gap, traders are signaling new

‚Ä¢ An up gap shows enthusiasm and a down gap shows

pessimism.

‚Ä¢ The news could be real (such as earnings) or a rumor

(such as a buyout or regulatory action).

‚Ä¢ If there is no news, the gap is an emotional response and

could indicate the market is irrational.

‚Ä¢ At times, gaps can show changes in market structure as

Reading candlestick charts

‚Ä¢ Candlestick patterns often consist of one to three candles.

‚Ä¢ Because they form in such a short time, they should only be used

for short-term analysis.

‚Ä¢ Patterns are often given descriptive names.

‚Ä¢ Three black crows, tweezers and homing pigeon are examples.
‚Ä¢ Three black crows are three down days that follow an uptrend.
‚Ä¢ Tweezers form when two bars have the same high or low but the

trend reverses.

‚Ä¢ Homing pigeon is a narrow range bar with a down close following

a wide range bar with a down close.

‚Ä¢ Like with traditional patterns, names are not as important as the

general outline.

‚Ä¢ Three consecutive down days after an uptrend signals a reversal.
‚Ä¢ Support or resistance indicate the direction of the trend could

change.

‚Ä¢ Narrow range forecasts increased volatility.
